Stage 03 · Onboard your first client

A client here is the business you're running leads for — the paying customer.

Before they sign

  1. Have the domain + warmup running — never promise day-of deliverables.
  2. Read MASTER_SALES_DOC.txt for the agency contract template.
  3. Decide the billing metric. Industry default: $150 per HOT lead forwarded.

Add the client in the database

Use POST /api/clients from the wizard, or via curl:

curl -X POST http://your-host/api/clients \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "name": "Johnson Roofing LLC",
    "niche": "Roofing",
    "city": "Austin",
    "region": "TX",
    "contact_name": "Mike Johnson",
    "contact_email": "mike@johnsonroofing.com",
    "retainer_cents": 250000,
    "start_date": "2025-01-15",
    "notes": "Storm-damage season focus; wants 40 leads per month",
    "days_per_week_target": 5
  }'

Field explanations

  • niche — must match one of the 24 in config/niches.json. The opener hooks differ per niche.
  • city / region / country — the hunters filter on these. Be exact.
  • contact_email — where the app forwards HOT leads. Use a personal mailbox, not info@.
  • retainer_cents — 250000 = $2500.00. Stored as cents to avoid float drift.
  • days_per_week_target — soft hint; the scheduler derives quotas from this.

Create a test client first

Don't point the system at a paying client until you've done a dry-run:

  1. Create a client called "Test Client."
  2. Trigger a hunt via POST /api/hunt with that client's id.
  3. Verify prospects show up in GET /api/clients/<id>/prospects.
  4. Send cold email to yourself only.
  5. Reply to that email. Within 15 minutes the reply poller should pick it up and mark it HOT.
  6. Confirm the forward email arrives at your personal mailbox.

Now add the real one

Once the dry-run works, deactivate the test client and create the paying one.
