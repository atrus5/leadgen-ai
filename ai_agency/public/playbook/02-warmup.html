Stage 02 · 14-day warmup

Two weeks. Don't skip this — new domains hit the spam folder 90% of the time without warmup.

What is warmup?

Mailbox providers (Gmail, Outlook, etc.) watch what happens to the messages a sender sends. If many of them go to spam, get marked as phishing, or get rejected, the sender lands on a blocklist. Warmup is the practice of gradually increasing volume while producing normal-looking responses: opens, replies, stars, marking-not-spam.

The day-by-day plan

After day 14

You can ramp up to whatever cap you've configured (default 40/day, configurable in config/settings.json's warmup.max_daily_after).

DNS — copy-paste

Add these to your registrar before you send email #1.

SPF

Type: TXT · Host: @ ·
Value: v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all

If using Zoho: replace with v=spf1 include:zoho.com ~all.

DKIM

Type: TXT · Host: google._domainkey ·
Value: (paste what Google Workspace shows under Admin → Apps → Gmail → Authenticate email)

DMARC

Type: TXT · Host: _dmarc ·
Value: v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@YOUR-DOMAIN.COM

MX (Google Workspace)

Type: MX · Host: @ ·
Priority 1: ASPMX.L.GOOGLE.COM
Priority 10: ALT1.ASPMX.L.GOOGLE.COM
Priority 10: ALT2.ASPMX.L.GOOGLE.COM

Troubleshooting

  • SPF returns "permerror"? You probably have two SPF records. Keep only one.
  • DKIM returns "fail"? The TXT record was likely cached before propagation finished. Wait 4 hours.
  • Outreach emails go directly to spam in week 1? You haven't set a DMARC policy — Gmail won't trust you yet.
