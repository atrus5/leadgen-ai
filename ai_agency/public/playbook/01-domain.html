Stage 01 · Pick and buy a domain

Estimated time: 15 minutes · Cost: $9–14/year

Why this matters

Cold-email deliverability is mostly decided in the first 30 days after you buy a domain. Two things make or break inbox placement:

  • Whether your domain and From address sound like a real business.
  • Whether you've set SPF, DKIM, and DMARC before sending your first mass email.

Pick a name

The domain becomes your outbound From identity. Recipients see it in From: hello@your-domain.com on every cold email. The wrong name will look like spam even before you send anything.

  • Good: short, pronounces like a real business, no hyphens, no "best-cheap-" prefixes. getacmeoutreach.com, tryacme.com, acmereach.com.
  • Avoid: brand-new TLDs (.xyz, .click, .top), hyphenated names, anything with numbers.
  • If you sell AI lead-gen, the name doesn't need to say "AI". acmereach.com is more credible than acme-ai-agency.io.

Where to buy

  • Namecheap — easiest onboarding for beginners. .com is ~$11/yr.
  • Cloudflare Registrar — at-cost pricing (~$9/yr for .com), great DNS panel.
  • Porkbun — cheaper for some TLDs.

What to do immediately after purchase

  1. Open the DNS / Advanced DNS panel.
  2. Add the records from stage 02's DNS section. SPF first, DKIM second, DMARC third, MX fourth.
  3. Verify the records with a tool like MXToolbox.

Common mistakes

  • Buying the domain but waiting until day 8 to set DNS. By then Google's reputation-startup clock is already running.
  • Using v=spf1 include:_spf.protonmail.com ~all when your actual mail comes from Google Workspace. Mismatched SPF = spam.
  • Enabling DMARC p=reject in week 1. Use p=none for the first 3 weeks, monitor, then graduate.

Save your work

When you finish, enter the domain in the wizard. The app will save it as the From address and start a warmup plan for it.
