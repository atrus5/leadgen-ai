Stage 04 · The nightly hunt

Every night at 02:10 (configurable), the scheduler runs both Apollo and Google Places for every active client.

How it works

For each active client, the scheduler calls:

  • hunters/apollo.py — runs a mixed people search against Apollo's REST API with the niche mapped to keyword filters and the city+region as the geographic constraint.
  • hunters/google_places.py — runs a Text Search on the Places API (New) for {niche} {city}, then folds in the first-place type for score weighting.

Results are normalized into prospect dicts and upserted with the unique constraint (client_id, external_id) so a business found via Apollo today won't be re-inserted by Places tomorrow.

Where each source shines

Apollo

Better for:

  • B2B services with verifiable decision makers: Software, Real Estate, Mortgage, Insurance, Legal, Marketing agencies.
  • Email is verified, not guessed. Good deliverability on day 1.
  • Costs Apollo credits. Free tier: 10k credits/month = enough for ~300 prospects.

Google Places

Better for:

  • Hyper-local services with little to no online presence: HVAC, Plumbing, Roofing, Auto mechanic, Pest control, Pool service, Painting.
  • Verified business addresses, hours, ratings, review counts.
  • Email is not exposed by Places — you'll need Hunter.io enrichment or open the owner's site to find the contact form.

Run the hunt manually

From the wizard's last stage, click "Run nightly now", or via curl:

curl -X POST http://your-host/api/jobs/run \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"name":"nightly_hunt"}'

What you see the next morning

The morning brief emails you a digest like:

Total new prospects inserted: 12

• Johnson Roofing (Roofing) via Apollo.io: 6/8 inserted
• Sunrise Dental (Dental) via Google Places: 4/12 inserted
• Mountain Mortgage (Mortgage) via Apollo.io: 2/7 inserted

Open LeadGen AI in your browser to review each prospect, choose which to contact, and tell the app to send a niche-tuned email.

What to do if "0 inserted" appears for a week

  • Your Apollo or Google Places API key is unset/expired.
  • The city lookup is too narrow. Try zooming out to a metro area.
  • The niche mapping isn't quite right — open config/niches.json and review the keywords.
  • The hunters hit the daily quota cap of 50 (Apollo) or 30 (Places).
